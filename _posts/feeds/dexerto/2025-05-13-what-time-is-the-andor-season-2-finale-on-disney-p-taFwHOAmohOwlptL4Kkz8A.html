If you’ve recovered from The Ghorman Massacre and Mon Mothma’s tense escape from the Senate, you’ll want to know when the next episode arrives, so here’s exactly when you can watch Andor Episodes 10, 11, and 12.

The Empire’s plans for Ghorman came to fruition in Episode 8, giving Mon Mothma the ammunition she needed to publicly call out Empire Palpatine, before finally meeting Cassian.

It’s what the entire season has been leading to, so I wouldn’t blame you for thinking it was the finale, but there are three more episodes to go.

Andor Season 2 Episodes 10-12, which include the finale, drop on Tuesday, May 13, at 6pm PT / 9pm ET on Disney Plus. If you’re outside the US, you won’t be able to see the finale until Wednesday, May 14.

Here’s a full breakdown of when it releases in your timezone:

  • 10pm BRT
  • 2am BST (Wednesday, May 14)
  • 3am CEST (Wednesday, May 14)
  • 4am EEST (Wednesday, May 14)
  • 6.30am IST (Wednesday, May 14)
  • 11am AEST (Wednesday, May 14)
  • 1pm NZST (Wednesday, May 14)

Also, be sure to check out our Andor Season 2 release schedule for the full details.

What will happen in Episodes 10-12?

Episodes 10-12 are set in 0 BBY, the same year as Rogue One and A New Hope. All we really know is that it ends with Cassian heading to the Ring of Kafrene, where we meet him in Rogue One.

So, we know where Cassian’s going, but we’re yet to find out what happens to Andor’s big players, such as Dedra, Luthen, Kleya, Lonnie, and Vel.

The trailer showed some scenes we’re yet to see, like Dedra pointing a blaster, Krennec overseeing the Death Star’s construction, and K-2SO ragdolling an Imperial soldier, but what actually happens is anyone’s guess.

Will there be an Andor S2 Episode 13?

No, Andor Season 2 Episode 12 is the final episode, and there won’t be a Season 3 either.

Although it was originally going to run for five seasons, showrunner Tony Gilroy realized it would take too long to make, so Season 2 covered four years instead.

What to watch after Andor Season 2

Rogue One is the only choice if you’re looking for a direct sequel to Andor, as it picks up almost immediately after the show ends and continues the story. Also written by Tony Gilroy, it’s where Cassian was first introduced, and it wraps up the story for a handful of the show’s characters.

There’s also Star Wars Rebels (hear me out). Sure, it’s made for kids and couldn’t be further from Andor in tone, but it has some incredible moments, shows more of the Rebellion, and, most importantly, one of the episodes continues Mon Mothma’s story after Episode 9.

After Cassian rescues Mon from the Senate, he’s told “they want to rewrite the story” by having her give a speech and arrive with a Yavin escort. Season 3 Episode 18 of Rebels shows what happens here, starting with the Ghost Crew listening to her denounce Palpatine following the Ghorman Massacre, then helping her escape the Empire, before she makes the speech Erskin mentions (he’s also in this episode).

Genevieve O’Reilly plays Mon Mothma in this episode, too, and while it is a kids’ show, it’s a nice palate cleanser after Andor’s emotional devastation.
