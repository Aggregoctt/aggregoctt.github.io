Move over Studio Ghibli images, there’s a new AI trend in town and not only does it move, it talks, too.

On May 20, Google unveiled Veo 3, an AI video generator that also includes audio, fully bringing creations to life.

We’ve come a long way from Will Smith eating noodles in silence. Unlike many other AI models, Veo 3 can generate video with sound and the results have mindblowing.

“Veo 3 excels from text and image prompting to real-world physics and accurate lip syncing,” Google DeepMind product vice president Eli Collins explained.

Since going live on Tuesday, Veo 3 creations have been going viral and the internet can’t stop talking about it.

Veo 3 AI videos take the internet by storm

One of the first videos to explode in popularity was created by X user Ari K, who highlighted the fact that AI generated people can now talk and posted his creation after just two hours of tinkering.

The video, which transitions from an old-fashioned movie to a cartoon to a science fiction film, shows characters excited at their ability to talk.

In another viral video, a user was able to generate a stand up comedy show using the simple prompt: “a man doing stand up comedy in a small venue tells a joke (include the joke in the dialogue).”

Another user was able to create a completely non-existent car show through the use of numerous prompts and it looks exactly like something you’d see watching a real… reel.

If that wasn’t wild enough, another viral post showed Cookie Monster destroying New York. As if that city doesn’t have enough problems!

Oh, and that viral one gorilla versus 100 dudes question was made it into a rave dance-off. So, there’s that.

If you want to try out Veo 3 for yourself, it’s not cheap. It’s only available for US subscribers of Google’s new Ultra subscription plan and it costs $249.99 per month.

That said, given the results, for any wannabe filmmakers out there, it could very well be worth the investment.
