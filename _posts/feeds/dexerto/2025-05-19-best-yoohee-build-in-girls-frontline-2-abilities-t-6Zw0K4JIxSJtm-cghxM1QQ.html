The best Yoohee build in GFL2 can drastically raise your Physical team comp’s damage, giving you the buffs needed to shred through PvE and PvP content.

Yoohee is the best Physical Support character in Girls Frontline 2 thanks to her amazing buffing abilities, which boost damage and reduce enemy defenses. As you can imagine, having her paired with single-target DPS units like Daiyan and Ullrid can quickly give you the tools needed to obliterate the tankiest bosses.

This deadly SSR Support is a must for every GFL2 player who plans to build a Physical damage team comp, so we recommend using your Collapse Pieces and Targeted Access Permissions on her limited banner.

If you’ve unlocked Yoohee or just want to know the best build, weapon, team comp, and more, we have you covered.

Best Yoohee build

To get the best Yoohee build, we recommend using the following Keys, attachments, weapons, and team:

  • Weapon: Sparkling Centerstage
  • Team: Yoohee, Daiyan, Ullrid, Suomi

Keys

  • Effort and Returns: When the active skill Improv consumes additional Cofectance Index, critical damage dealt is increased.
  • Infectious Emotions: After using the active skill Soul of Dance to apply Stun to an enemy target, Yoohee applies Movement Denied to the target when Stun is removed.
  • Exquisite Choreography: When Never Give Up is in effect, Yoohee’s attack is increased

Best attachments

  • Ballistic Boost: When dealing Physical damage, the damage is increased by 20%.
  • Phase Strike: Deals 15% increased damage to targets with Phase Element debuffs. This set is great for players who want to avoid grinding and have a set that can be used on multiple characters.

Yoohee will release on May 22, 2025, and her banner will be available until June 11, 2025.

This means players have plenty of time to pull on the limited banner. She’s also available alongside Littara and Ksenia, two SR units that have a rate-up boost.

Best weapon

The best Yoohee weapon is Sparkling Centerstage. This weapon is tailor-made for Yoohee, as it gives her one random buff at the start of her turn for one turn if her HP is full. Additionally, Allied units ignore 10% of the target’s defense when performing a Physical attack.

Yoohee’s active Physical attacks also ignore 4% of the target’s defense, depending on the number of buffs applied to other allied units, up to a maximum of 20/24/28/32/36/40%. As Yoohee’s abilities are tailored around buffing her team, the idol will constantly be able to capitalize on the added DPS offered from Sparkling Centerstage.

If you haven’t unlocked Yoohee’s signature weapon, you can always use the Planeta or Golden Melody from the Neural Integration shop.

The best free-to-play option is the Planeta, which increases damage whenever a kill is made or the unit’s Extra Action ends.

Best Yoohee team

The current best Yoohee team comp in the global version of GFL2 is the following:

  • Yoohee (Support/buff)
  • Daiyan (DPS)
  • Ullrid (DPS)
  • Suomi (Support/Healer)

This team is the best Physical team comp in the current version of GFL2. The main synergies here areDaiyan + Ullrid, as whenever these units deal Physical damage, Yoohee gains additional points of Confectance Index.

This enables the idol to constantly unleash her Improv skill, which consumes additional Cofectance points to ignore 30% of the target’s defence.

If Yoohee has 3 or more points of Confectance Index, consumes Confectance Index to cause this attack to ignore part of the target’s defense. Both Daiyan and Ullrid greatly benefit from Yoohee’s Graceful Pirouette, as it raises their Critical Damage and adds Defense Shredding when dealing Physical damage to the target.

If an enemy’s defense has been reduced by more than 100%, it increases the damage dealt to the target even further. We rounded off the team with Suomi, as her healing shields will keep all your units alive during the toughest fights.

While this team comp doesn’t beat the current meta Burn team, Yoohee is a must if you wish to begin making the best GFL2 Physical team.
